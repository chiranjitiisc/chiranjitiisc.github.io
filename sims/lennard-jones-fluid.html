What am I looking at?

N particles interact through the Lennard-Jones 12-6 potential, V(r) = 4ε[(σ/r)12 − (σ/r)6], in reduced units (σ = ε = m = kB = 1). Newton's equations are integrated with the velocity-Verlet algorithm (Δt* = 0.004), the box uses periodic boundary conditions with the minimum-image convention, and the potential is cut off and shifted at rc = 2.5σ — exactly the ingredients of a research MD code.

In NVE the system is isolated: total energy E should stay constant (watch the white line — small wiggles in KE and PE mirror each other). In NVT a Berendsen-type velocity-rescaling thermostat gently drives the kinetic temperature toward the target. The kinetic temperature is computed from equipartition, T* = 2·KE / (2N − 2) (two degrees of freedom per particle in 2D, minus the frozen centre-of-mass motion).

Things to try:
  • Melt a crystal. Load the "Cold crystal" preset, run in NVT, then slowly raise T* past ~0.4 and watch the lattice lose order.
  • Test energy conservation. Switch to NVE and check that E/N stays flat while KE and PE trade places.
  • Quench a gas. Load "Gas", then set T* = 0.1 in NVT — droplets condense as the thermostat removes kinetic energy.
  • Break the integrator. Raise sim speed to max at high density and T*; if you crank conditions hard enough you may see E/N drift — a lesson in time-step stability.
