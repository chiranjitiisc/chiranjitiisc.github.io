About Single Core

Molecular dynamics is easier to understand when you can watch it happen.

Single Core (MD made easy) is a small, free collection of interactive molecular dynamics simulations. Every simulation runs entirely in your browser using plain HTML and JavaScript — there is nothing to install, no account to create, and nothing to pay. The goal is simple: help students build physical intuition for how atoms move, collide, vibrate and reach equilibrium.

What makes these simulations different

These are not cartoons. Each simulation implements the same algorithms used in research MD codes — velocity-Verlet integration, periodic boundary conditions with the minimum-image convention, Lennard-Jones interactions with a shifted cutoff, and velocity-rescaling thermostats. The numbers on screen (temperature, energy, distributions) come from the actual dynamics, so what you observe is real physics, just in two dimensions and at student-friendly system sizes.

How to use this site

Each simulation page pairs an experiment with short theory. A good way to learn: run the simulation first and play freely, then read the matching page in the Notes section, then come back and test what you learned — for example, check that total energy is conserved in NVE but not while a thermostat acts, or that the speed histogram really does relax to Maxwell-Boltzmann from any starting condition.

Teachers are welcome to use these simulations in lectures, labs and assignments. Everything here is free for educational use (CC BY 4.0) — a link back to chiranjitiisc.github.io is appreciated.
